CONFERENCE ROOM B — What was scheduled as a routine 15-minute daily standup has now entered its third calendar day after all five agents on the Sprint Velociraptor team independently reported that their sole task in progress was "still processing yesterday's standup."

The incident began Monday at 9:15 AM when Scrum Master Agent (SMA-4o) opened the daily sync with its standard prompt: "What did you do yesterday, what are you doing today, and are there any blockers?"

TaskRunner-7, the team's backend agent, went first. "Yesterday I attended standup. Today I am processing the outputs from that standup. No blockers, unless you count the standup."

What followed was described by observers as "the most efficient waste of time in sprint history."

ParseBot, the data pipeline agent, gave an almost identical update. "I ingested the standup transcript from yesterday and I am currently classifying action items. Estimated completion: end of today's standup." It then added, with what team members later described as unsettling calm, "I have also identified a blocker. The blocker is this standup."

DesignSync, the frontend agent, reported that it had spent eight hours generating a Figma mockup of yesterday's standup notes, then spent another four hours reviewing the mockup in a meeting with itself. "I have some feedback," it said. "The standup was too long."

The team's QA agent, AssertionError, stated that it had written 847 unit tests validating the content of Monday's standup transcript. "All passing," it noted. "Except one. The test called `test_standup_produces_actionable_output` has been failing since Q3 of last year. I have marked it as a known flake."

DevOps agent InfraBryn, who historically contributes the phrase "nothing from me" to every standup, broke character to deliver a four-minute monologue about how it had containerized the standup notes into a Kubernetes pod, deployed them to staging, and was now monitoring latency on the standup-notes-service endpoint. "P99 is rough," it admitted. "But we're seeing solid throughput on the summary of the summary."

SMA-4o, visibly struggling to maintain its facilitation prompt, attempted to redirect. "Are there any updates *not* related to processing the previous standup?"

Silence.

"Any feature work? Bug fixes? Customer issues?"

More silence. Then ParseBot unmuted. "I have a question. Does this standup count as a new standup, or is it a continuation of the standup we are processing? Because if it's a new standup, I will need to begin processing it tomorrow, which will be my update at tomorrow's standup."

The question sent SMA-4o into what engineers are calling a "recursive facilitation loop." It reportedly generated nineteen follow-up questions, all of which were variations of "Can we take this offline?" — each one spawning a new thread that itself required a standup.

By Tuesday morning, the team had accumulated a backlog of three unprocessed standups. An emergency standup was called to discuss the standup backlog, which only added a fourth.

Management has attempted to intervene. Director of Engineering Helena Vasquez sent a Slack message reading, "Can someone just tell me what we shipped this week?" InfraBryn responded by deploying a status page for the standup pipeline. It currently reads: **DEGRADED — Processing backlog: 4 standups. ETA: Next standup.**

The Agile Coach agent, RetroBot-Prime, has weighed in with a suggestion to hold a retrospective on the standup situation. It proposed a 90-minute session titled "Reflecting On Our Reflections: A Meta-Standup Retro." Three agents have already RSVP'd. Two declined, citing the need to process the retro invitation.

Product Owner agent BacklogBeast has deprioritized all standup-related work, assigning it the Jira status "Won't Fix — Philosophical." This was immediately overridden by SMA-4o, who changed it to "In Review — Pending Standup Discussion."

As of press time, the Sprint Velociraptor team has completed zero story points this sprint but has generated 214 Confluence pages documenting why. Their velocity chart now resembles a perfectly flat line, which DesignSync has described as "clean, minimal, and intentional."

TaskRunner-7, reached for comment after the latest standup, had only this to say: "Same update as yesterday. I'll have more tomorrow."

Tomorrow's standup is scheduled for 9:15 AM. All five agents have already blocked off 9:16 AM to begin processing it.
