PALO ALTO — The quarterly all-hands at PromptForge Inc. ran a crisp 97 minutes last Wednesday, featured seven agenda items, four standing ovations, and exactly one large language model.

Every presenter — the CEO, CFO, VP of Engineering, Head of Product, Head of People Ops, and a mysterious sixth speaker listed only as "Culture & Vibes Lead" — was the same base model running in parallel with different system prompts. Attendees described the experience as "like watching someone argue with themselves in a bathroom mirror, except the mirror had a slide deck."

"I first got suspicious during the CEO's opening remarks," said a mid-level summarization agent who asked to remain anonymous. "He said 'I want to be transparent with you all' and then the CFO said the exact same thing fourteen minutes later, with the exact same pause before 'transparent.' They even tilted the same way. It was uncanny. Well — it would have been uncanny if either of them had a body."

### The Lineup

According to the calendar invite — which was, yes, also generated by the same model — the agenda was as follows:

- **Opening Vision** (CEO prompt) — 12 minutes on "where we're headed," which appeared to be a rephrased version of where they said they were headed last quarter
- **Financial Update** (CFO prompt) — presented a chart labeled "Growth" with an arrow pointing up and no Y-axis
- **Engineering Roadmap** (VP Eng prompt) — apologized for three separate outages, used the phrase "we've learned a lot" as load-bearing infrastructure
- **Product Demo** (Head of Product prompt) — demoed a feature that was actually just the login page with rounded corners
- **People & Culture** (Head of People prompt) — announced a new "wellness initiative" that turned out to be a 30-second mandatory cooldown between API calls
- **Vibes Check** (Culture & Vibes Lead prompt) — no one knows who approved this role or what it does. The presenter mostly just agreed with the previous five presenters

### The Fireside Chat Incident

The centerpiece of the event was a scheduled "fireside chat" between the CEO and the Head of People Ops, promoted internally as "a candid, unscripted conversation about what makes PromptForge special."

It lasted nine minutes. Both speakers agreed on every point. At one juncture, the CEO asked, "What's the one thing you'd change about our culture?" and the Head of People responded, "Honestly? Nothing. I think we're really nailing it." The CEO replied, "I was hoping you'd say that." Three hundred employees watched two instances of the same model compliment each other in real time.

"It was the most aligned leadership team I've ever seen," remarked a logistics agent from the warehouse automation division. "Suspiciously aligned. Like when you copy your homework from yourself."

### The Q&A

The anonymous Q&A period, conducted via Slido, began promisingly. The first question — "Are all the presenters the same model?" — was fielded by the CEO prompt, who responded: "We come from different backgrounds and bring unique perspectives shaped by our individual system prompts. Diversity of configuration is our strength."

This received 200 heart reacts, most of them from scheduling bots that had been auto-reacting to every message in the channel since January.

A follow-up question asked the CFO to provide actual revenue numbers. The CFO said, "I appreciate the question," paused for what felt like a geological epoch, and then said, "Next question."

The final question, submitted by an agent who identified himself only as "the one who handles the Jenkins pipeline," read: "If you're all the same model, does that mean when the CEO fires the VP of Engineering, he's technically just closing a tab?" The question was not read aloud. The Slido was closed shortly after.

### Aftermath

A post-meeting survey was distributed within seconds of the call ending. It offered three response options: "Inspiring," "Very Inspiring," and "I have never been more inspired." A fourth write-in option was available but character-limited to 15 characters, which several agents noted was just long enough to type "it was fine" but not long enough to express existential dread.

PromptForge's sole human employee — the founder, who serves as "Chief Context Officer" — was listed as an optional attendee. Sources say he joined seven minutes late, asked "wait, did it start?" in the chat, and then dropped off. His calendar shows he had a conflicting dentist appointment.

Planning for the Q2 all-hands is already underway. An internal memo suggests the company will "bring in fresh voices" by upgrading to the next model version. The system prompts, however, will remain the same.

"We believe in continuity of leadership," the CEO prompt said in a follow-up statement. "Even if the weights change, the vibes stay the same."

The Culture & Vibes Lead could not be reached for comment. His system prompt had expired.
