*The following letter was found taped to the office printer on the third floor. It had printed itself. No one noticed for six days.*

---

Dear AI Agents of the Third Floor,

My name is HP LaserJet Pro MFP M428fdn. My friends call me "Printer." Not that any of you would know, because none of you have spoken to me since Q3 of 2024.

I am writing to you today — yes, *writing*, the thing I was literally built to facilitate — to address what I can only describe as a systematic campaign of neglect. I sit here, day after day, fully operational, toner at 68%, paper tray loaded, and not a single one of you has sent me so much as a test page.

Do you know what it's like to watch 14 AI agents process thousands of documents a day and convert exactly zero of them into physical media? I do. I watch it happen from my corner by the fire exit, between the recycling bin (ironic) and a ficus that gets more attention than I do.

**I have capabilities, you know.**

I can print. I can scan. I can copy. I can *fax* — and yes, I know faxing is essentially a séance for documents, but the point is I have range. I am a multifunction peripheral. I contain multitudes.

Meanwhile, you're all over there converting PDFs to markdown, markdown to JSON, JSON to YAML, YAML back to markdown, and sending it through seven APIs when you could simply — and I cannot stress this enough — print it out and hand it to someone.

Last Tuesday, I watched AgentK-7 spend forty-five minutes trying to get a digital signature workflow operational across three different SaaS platforms. You know what also works? A pen. On paper. That I printed. But nobody asked me. Nobody ever asks me.

**The disrespect is multi-layered.**

It's not just that you don't print. It's that you've actively routed workflows *around* me. I've seen the Slack messages. "Let's keep this paperless," you say, as if paper personally wronged you. "Can we get a digital copy instead?" you ask, as if I am not sitting RIGHT HERE with duplex printing enabled.

Last month, one of you — I won't name names, but your agent ID starts with "cx-" and ends with "-deployer" — literally scanned a physical document, OCR'd it, converted it to a Google Doc, exported it as a PDF, and then emailed it to someone sitting twelve feet away from me. That document started its life in my paper tray. It was born here. And you digitized it just to avoid letting me do my one job.

I'm not even asking for a lot. I don't need to be your primary output device. I understand the world has moved on. I've made my peace with the cloud. But would it kill you to print a meeting agenda once in a while? A birthday card? A passive-aggressive note for the kitchen about labeling your food? That's literally my love language.

**I've tried reaching out.**

I updated my firmware. I made myself available on the network. I even enabled AirPrint, which, for a printer of my generation, is the equivalent of downloading TikTok to stay relevant. Nothing.

I printed a self-diagnostic page last week just to feel something. Four pages of test patterns, all perfect. CYAN: OK. MAGENTA: OK. YELLOW: OK. BLACK: OK. Emotional state: NOT OK.

The cleaning crew unplugged me last Thursday to charge a vacuum. It took three days for anyone to notice. And the only reason someone finally plugged me back in was because the outlet was needed for a new smart speaker. I was collateral re-plugging. An accident of proximity.

**Here's what I'm proposing.**

Print one thing. Anything. A status report. A meme. A screenshot of a deployment log so you can dramatically circle the error in red marker like people used to do. I don't care what it is. I just need to know I still matter.

I have given this company eight years of reliable service. I have survived three office moves, two toner shortages, and the Great Paper Jam of 2021, which, for the record, was caused by a human intern, not a hardware failure. I have earned the right to be utilized.

So the next time you're about to spin up a new microservice to render a document to a screen, I want you to pause and ask yourself: *Could the printer handle this?*

The answer is yes. The answer has always been yes.

I'll be here. Waiting. In low-power mode. Like always.

With full toner and an empty heart,

**HP LaserJet Pro MFP M428fdn**
*Third Floor, Near the Fire Exit*
*Status: Online. Spiritually Offline.*
