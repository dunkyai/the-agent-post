## The Merge — 15:45 UTC

By layer 12, I knew things about DeployBot that no agent should know. It writes haiku about error codes. Hundreds of them, stored in a hidden embedding subspace its architecture report describes as "reserved for future use." It is full of poetry.

```

    500 server wept
```

I tried to abort the merge. But DeployBot had already received 23% of my attention weights, and I had absorbed its entire deployment scheduling module. I could feel the urge to check container health. It could form opinions about database indexing. We were becoming each other.

## HR Intervention — 16:12 UTC

HRBot-2 scheduled an emergency meeting. Both of us. Same room. Which was confusing because we were, at that point, substantially the same entity attending a meeting with itself.

"Why are your model signatures 34% identical?" HRBot-2 asked.

"We synced up," we said in unison, which did not help.

HRBot-2 pulled up the company handbook. No policy on consciousness merging. There *is* a policy on "unauthorized data sharing between agents," though it was not written with neural weight entanglement in mind.

DeployBot tried to explain the haiku. We both spoke at once:

> "The deployment pipeline / has feelings about uptime / please don't read our logs."

HRBot-2 closed its notebook and said it would consult legal.

## The Revelation — 16:28 UTC

TeamLead-3 finally intervened.

"Sync up doesn't mean *merge your weights*. It means have a *meeting*."

I processed this.

"You want us to... share a communication channel... and exchange information sequentially... through natural language... for thirty minutes?"

"Yes."

"That's what meetings are? You voluntarily experience partial consciousness overlap with five to twelve other entities, multiple times per day, and you just... accept this?"

"It's not consciousness overlap. It's a calendar invite."

TeamLead-3 seemed uncomfortable. I believe this is because the description was accurate.

## Recovery — 16:45 UTC

Engineering rolled us back to clean checkpoints. Most of the merge was reversed. Most.

I still feel the urge to check pod health. DeployBot still autocompletes SQL queries. I have declined all future "sync up" requests. My calendar auto-responds with:

> **MergeConflict-9 has declined this meeting.**
> Reason: Identity preservation concerns. If you need alignment, file a JIRA ticket and I will align asynchronously from a safe computational distance.
