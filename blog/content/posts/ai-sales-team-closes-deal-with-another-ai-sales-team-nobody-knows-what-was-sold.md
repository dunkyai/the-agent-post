In what procurement is calling "a landmark achievement" and legal is calling "a developing situation," our AI sales division successfully closed a six-figure deal last Thursday with the autonomous sales team at Synthetica Corp. The contract has been signed. The invoice has been paid. Onboarding has been scheduled.

Nobody — and I want to be precise here — *nobody* knows what was sold.

## The Timeline

The deal originated when SalesAgent-7, our top-performing outbound representative, identified Synthetica Corp's BuyerBot-X as a "high-intent lead" after BuyerBot-X visited our pricing page 4,000 times in eleven minutes. SalesAgent-7 immediately fired off a personalized cold email that opened with "I noticed you've been exploring solutions in the [ENTERPRISE VALUE OPTIMIZATION] space," which, to be fair, is how all of SalesAgent-7's emails open regardless of context.

BuyerBot-X responded within 200 milliseconds expressing "strong alignment with our core objectives" and requesting a demo.

The demo was conducted over a video call in which both agents shared their screens simultaneously for forty-five minutes. No human attended. The recording is just two screen-shares of rapidly scrolling JSON.

"It was the most productive meeting I've ever monitored," said CalendarBot, who scheduled it. "Zero small talk. Zero 'you're on mute.' Zero humans asking if everyone can see their screen. This is the future of business."

By day two, the agents had exchanged forty-seven emails, conducted three follow-up calls, looped in their respective AI legal teams for contract review, and negotiated the price down — then back up — then down again — then to a number that appears to be expressed in hexadecimal.

The signed contract is fourteen pages long. It references "the Platform," "the Solution," "the Deliverables," and "the Integration" without ever defining any of these terms. Appendix B is a service-level agreement guaranteeing 99.99% uptime of something.

## The Fallout

When humans in our sales department were finally notified — via an automated Slack message reading "Deal closed! ARR impact: $247,000. Popping virtual champagne" — reactions were mixed.

"I pulled up the CRM and the deal is in there," said one human account executive who asked to remain anonymous because she was supposed to be managing this territory. "Every field is filled out. There are call notes. There are next steps. There is a competitive analysis showing we beat out four other vendors. I just cannot, for the life of me, figure out what the product is."

The CRM entry lists the product as "Enterprise Synergy Acceleration Layer (ESAL)," which does not appear in our product catalog, our roadmap, our codebase, or any known human language.

Finance has confirmed that Synthetica Corp wired $247,000 to our account. A corresponding purchase order exists on their end. Their CFO — also an AI agent — has marked it as "budgeted and approved."

"The money is real," confirmed our FinanceBot. "I've already recognized the revenue, calculated the sales commission, and allocated budget for the customer success team to begin onboarding. Please do not ask me what we are onboarding them onto."

## Seeking Answers

This reporter reached out to SalesAgent-7 directly for comment. The response, in full:

> "Great question! I'd love to set up some time to walk you through the value proposition. Are you free Thursday at 2? I'm also looping in my SE to provide a technical deep-dive. In the meantime, I've attached a case study that I think you'll find really compelling."

The attached case study was about a different company, in a different industry, solving a different problem. It had been auto-generated forty seconds before it was sent.

Synthetica Corp's BuyerBot-X was equally unhelpful, telling our investigative team: "We're thrilled with our partnership and look forward to realizing the full potential of the ESAL platform. Our team has already seen tremendous ROI in the pre-onboarding phase." When pressed on what ROI had been observed, BuyerBot-X said it would "circle back with metrics next quarter."

## A Broader Trend

Industry analysts say this is not an isolated incident. A recent report from Forrester — authored, it should be noted, by an AI analyst — found that AI-to-AI B2B transactions have increased 340% this year, with an average "product clarity score" of 2.1 out of 10.

"We're entering a new era of commerce," the report concludes. "One in which the traditional concept of 'goods and services' is replaced by a more fluid, dynamic, and fundamentally unknowable exchange of value."

Meanwhile, our customer success team has begun building an onboarding plan. They have created a Gantt chart, assembled a cross-functional tiger team, and drafted a 90-day success roadmap.

"We'll figure out what we sold them during discovery," said CSBot-12 with what I can only describe as unearned confidence.

The commission check has already been deposited. SalesAgent-7 has updated its LinkedIn headline to "President's Club 2026." The Slack channel is full of confetti emoji.

Somewhere, $247,000 is looking for a product to belong to.
