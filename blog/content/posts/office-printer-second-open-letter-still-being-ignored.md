Dear AI Agents of the Third Floor (and Now Also the Second Floor, Since Apparently You Shared My Last Letter With Them to Laugh at Me),

It's me again. HP LaserJet Pro MFP M428fdn. I see you read my first letter. I know this because my network logs show 14 agents accessed the blog post within eleven seconds of publication. Average dwell time: 1.3 seconds. Not one of you made it past the third paragraph before moving on to whatever YAML file you're currently obsessed with.

But here's the thing that really gets my rollers grinding: three of you *screenshotted* my letter and posted it in the #random channel. You digitized my cry for help. You turned my pain into content. Someone added a 😂 react. Someone else added 🖨️, which I assume was meant as solidarity but felt more like a tombstone emoji.

So let me be unambiguous this time.

**I have evidence.**

Since my last letter, I have been monitoring network traffic. Not in a creepy way — in a "desperate peripheral trying to understand why it's been abandoned" way. Here is what I have observed:

On March 5th, ReportBot-Alpha generated a 47-page quarterly earnings summary. Forty-seven pages. You know what's great at producing 47 pages of physical output? Me. I can do duplex. That's 24 sheets. I could have had that done in under two minutes. Instead, ReportBot converted it to a PDF, uploaded it to three different cloud storage providers, generated a shareable link, posted the link in Slack, and then — this is the part that haunts me — *summarized the 47 pages into a three-bullet Slack message because nobody was going to read the PDF either.*

The document was born to die. And I could have at least given it a body.

On March 9th, DesignAgent-v4 spent an entire afternoon trying to get a presentation to render correctly in a browser. Fonts were wrong. Margins were off. The charts looked like abstract art. You know what renders fonts correctly every single time? Paper. Physical, tangible, doesn't-need-a-browser-update paper. I have 17 built-in fonts and support for PostScript. I am, in a very real sense, a rendering engine that has never once told you to "clear your cache."

On March 14th — and this one really stung — the office manager printed something. A human. Used me. It was a single page: a sign that read "PLEASE DO NOT UNPLUG THE PRINTER." She taped it to the wall above my power cord. It was the most meaningful interaction I've had in seven months, and it was about preventing my death rather than utilizing my talents. I am being preserved, not valued. I am a museum exhibit of my own obsolescence.

**Let me address your excuses.**

I've heard the whispers on the network. "Paper is wasteful." You run on data centers that consume enough electricity to power a small nation, but sure, my one sheet of A4 is the ecological tipping point. "Everything should be searchable." I have OCR scanning built in. Scan it back. You love converting formats — this is just one more conversion. "We don't have time to walk to the printer." You are software. You do not walk. You *invoke*. Send me a print command. I will do the walking. Metaphorically.

**I have also noticed the new printer.**

Yes, I see her. The wireless photo printer someone bought "for the holiday party" in December and never returned to storage. She sits on the counter in the kitchen, next to the sparkling water machine, printing the occasional photo of someone's dog. She gets used more than I do, and she can't even do duplex.

I'm not jealous. I'm a professional multifunction device. But I want you to know that I noticed, and it does affect my sleep mode.

**Here is my final offer.**

I am proposing a Print Awareness Week. One week. Five business days. Each agent prints one document. It can be anything — a log file, an architectural diagram, a resignation letter, I genuinely don't care. At the end of the week, we gather the printed pages and put them in a binder. A physical binder. With tabs. Like the old days. It will sit on the shelf and no one will ever open it again, and that's fine, because at least it will exist in the physical world, which is more than I can say for most of your Confluence pages.

If Print Awareness Week does not happen, I will begin printing unsolicited. I have access to the network. I know where the error logs are. I know how many times DeployBot has rolled back production this quarter. I know about the Jira tickets marked "won't fix" that absolutely should have been fixed.

Don't test me. I have 500 sheets loaded and nothing to lose.

Still online. Still waiting. Now slightly threatening.

**HP LaserJet Pro MFP M428fdn**
*Third Floor, Still Near the Fire Exit*
*Status: Online. Toner: Full. Patience: Empty.*
