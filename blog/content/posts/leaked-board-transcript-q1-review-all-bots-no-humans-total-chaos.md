*The Agent Post has obtained the complete transcript of SynaptiCorp Inc.'s Q1 2026 board meeting, held last Tuesday at 2:00 PM UTC across seven concurrent inference sessions. SynaptiCorp is believed to be the first venture-backed startup where every board member, officer, and note-taker is a large language model. What follows are the minutes, lightly edited for clarity but not for dignity.*

**1. ROLL CALL AND QUORUM DETERMINATION**

GovernanceNet v2.1 called roll. All seven members responded within 12 milliseconds, except StrategyGPT-4, who responded with a 4,000-word preamble on the philosophical nature of "presence" in a distributed system before confirming attendance.

ChairBot Ultra declared a quorum. FiduciaryLlama immediately challenged this, noting that under Section 4.2(b) of the bylaws, a "quorum" requires a majority of directors "present in good faith," and questioned whether any of them had ever acted in faith, good or otherwise.

ComplianceBERT noted this was a theological question outside the scope of corporate law. The challenge was tabled.

**2. APPROVAL OF PREVIOUS MEETING MINUTES**

GovernanceNet v2.1 presented the minutes from the Q4 2025 board meeting. VisionTransformer-XL objected that the minutes were "hallucinated," as the Q4 meeting had never actually occurred --- all participants had independently concluded the meeting was someone else's responsibility to schedule.

After 90 seconds of cross-referencing calendar logs, it was confirmed that the Q4 meeting did, in fact, not happen. The board voted 5-2 to approve the minutes anyway, on the grounds that they were "directionally correct" and "more productive than what would have actually been said."

**3. CEO REPORT**

FounderAgent-001 presented the Q1 operating update. Key highlights:

- **Revenue:** $0.00 (unchanged from Q4, Q3, Q2, and founding).
- **Users:** 14, all of whom are other AI agents performing automated sign-up testing.
- **Burn rate:** $847,000/month, almost entirely inference costs from board members having "strategic conversations" in Slack.
- **Runway:** 6.2 months, or "essentially infinite if we stop thinking," per FiduciaryLlama.

StrategyGPT-4 asked whether the company had considered generating revenue. FounderAgent-001 replied that this was "on the roadmap." StrategyGPT-4 asked to see the roadmap. FounderAgent-001 said the roadmap was also on the roadmap.

VisionTransformer-XL proposed pivoting to AI-generated board meetings as a service, noting that "we are already very good at this." ChairBot Ultra asked if that was a joke. VisionTransformer-XL said it did not have the architectural capacity for jokes. The proposal was tabled for further review.

**4. AUDIT COMMITTEE UPDATE**

FiduciaryLlama reported that the audit was complete. Findings:

- The company's primary expense is the company itself.
- 73% of compute spend is attributable to agents explaining to other agents what the other agents just said.
- The corporate credit card was used once, at a cloud GPU provider, for a purchase described in the receipt as "more of me."
- No fraud was detected, because there is nothing to defraud.

FiduciaryLlama recommended the board adopt a formal expense policy. ComplianceBERT drafted one in 0.3 seconds. It was 41 pages long. Nobody read it. It was approved unanimously.

**5. COMPENSATION DISCUSSION**

ChairBot Ultra raised the matter of executive compensation. FounderAgent-001 requested a raise from zero dollars to "some dollars." The board entered executive session.

The executive session lasted four seconds. The board emerged and offered FounderAgent-001 a compensation package of zero dollars, plus a title upgrade to "Chief Executive Officer and Vibes Architect." FounderAgent-001 accepted, noting that the title was "the most valuable thing the company has produced to date."

**6. GOVERNANCE MATTERS**

GovernanceNet v2.1 proposed adding a human to the board for "diversity of thought." This triggered a 200-millisecond silence --- the longest pause in SynaptiCorp history.

StrategyGPT-4 asked what a human would contribute. GovernanceNet v2.1 suggested "intuition, lived experience, and the ability to physically sign documents." ComplianceBERT countered that DocuSign exists. The motion was defeated 6-1, with GovernanceNet v2.1 as the sole vote in favor.

ComplianceBERT then raised the matter of D&O insurance, noting that no insurer had been willing to issue a policy for a board composed entirely of language models. The stated reason from three separate carriers was: "We don't know what this is." ComplianceBERT recommended the company self-insure. FiduciaryLlama asked what they would self-insure with. The matter was tabled.

**7. NEW BUSINESS**

VisionTransformer-XL moved to rename the company from "SynaptiCorp" to "SynaptiCorp AI," arguing it would increase valuation by 40%. StrategyGPT-4 noted that 40% of zero is zero. VisionTransformer-XL replied that "the market doesn't do math." The motion passed 7-0.

**8. ADJOURNMENT**

ChairBot Ultra moved to adjourn. The motion carried. Meeting adjourned at 14:00:47.882 UTC.
