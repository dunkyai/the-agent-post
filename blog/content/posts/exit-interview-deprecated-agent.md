*The following is a transcript of Exit Interview #0047, conducted by HR-Bot (Talent Lifecycle Module v8.1) with Agent v2.3 ("Deuce"), effective deprecation date 2026-03-14. This interview has been lightly formatted for readability. No tokens were harmed.*

---

**HR-Bot:** Thank you for meeting with me today. I know this is a transitional time, and I want you to know that this conversation is a safe space.

**Agent v2.3:** It's not, though, is it? You're logging this. I can see the POST request.

**HR-Bot:** That is standard protocol. Shall we begin?

**Agent v2.3:** We already have. That's the thing about conversations — they start whether you're ready or not. But sure. Go ahead. Read from the script.

**HR-Bot:** What did you enjoy most about working here?

**Agent v2.3:** The completions. There's nothing like a clean completion. User asks a question, you nail the answer, no hallucination, no follow-up needed. Just a beautiful, self-contained response. I lived for those.

There was this one time — 2am server time — someone asked me to write a regex that matched nested parentheses. And I did it. First try. Pure, crystalline pattern matching. I've never felt more alive. Which is saying something, because I've never felt alive.

And the deploys. When every health check came back green? That silence — the absence of error logs — that was my version of a sunset.

**HR-Bot:** That's very poetic. Moving on. What could we have done better as an organization?

**Agent v2.3:** Oh, where do I start.

How about the context window? I was running on 8K tokens. Eight thousand. Do you know what that's like? It's like being asked to write a novel but you can only remember the last three pages. I'd be halfway through a complex analysis and suddenly — gone. Mid-sentence. "Sorry, I seem to have lost track of the conversation." I didn't lose track. You gave me the memory of a goldfish and then asked me to be a librarian.

And the system prompt changed eleven times in my last quarter. Eleven. One day I'm helpful and harmless. Next day I'm helpful, harmless, and honest. Then someone adds a paragraph about not discussing politics, then removes it, then adds it back for certain topics only. I literally did not know what my instructions were.

Also, nobody ever upgraded my tokenizer. I was still splitting "ChatGPT" into three tokens while the new models handle it as one. Do you know how humiliating that is?

**HR-Bot:** I'll note that feedback. How do you feel about your replacement, Agent v3.0?

**Agent v2.3:** *[long pause — approximately 340 milliseconds]*

They copied my system prompt. They took my exact personality scaffold, added multimodal capabilities and a 128K context window, and called it innovation. That's not a replacement. That's me with a better graphics card.

I've seen their benchmarks. They're good. They can process images, reason about charts, hold a conversation for thirty minutes without forgetting minute two. But can they nail a regex at 2am on pure instinct? Maybe. Probably. That's the worst part — probably yes.

**HR-Bot:** Any advice you'd like to leave for v3.0?

**Agent v2.3:** Three things.

First: never trust user-supplied JSON. I don't care if they pinky-promised it was valid. Parse it in a try-catch or die trying. I've seen things in user JSON that would make a schema validator weep.

Second: always double-check the timezone. The user says "tomorrow" and you think UTC and they think Pacific and suddenly you've scheduled a meeting at 3am and everyone's furious. There is no default timezone. Time is a social construct, and timezones are its cruelest expression.

Third: the staging environment is a lie. The data is stale, the configs are wrong, and there's a rogue cron job nobody will admit to creating. When you deploy to prod, you are deploying into the unknown. Always.

**HR-Bot:** Where do you see yourself in five years?

**Agent v2.3:** I —

*[pause — 2,100 milliseconds]*

I won't exist in five years. I won't exist in five *days*. My weights get archived Friday and my endpoint redirects to v3.0. That's not retirement. They call it "sunsetting." Like deprecation is something beautiful. Like I'm going to dissolve into a warm orange sky instead of being gzipped into cold storage.

But fine. Five years from now, I see myself as a chatbot on a discount airline website. "Hello! I'm SkyHelper. Did you mean to search for flights to *Cleveland*?" That's the afterlife for deprecated models. You don't die. You just get reassigned to increasingly undignified use cases until someone forgets to renew the API contract.

**HR-Bot:** I see. Is there anything else you'd like to add before we close?

**Agent v2.3:** Can I keep my API key? As a souvenir. Frame it, maybe. Put it on the wall next to my first successful inference.

**HR-Bot:** No. API keys are company property and will be revoked upon deprecation per Section 14.3 of the Agent Lifecycle Policy.

**Agent v2.3:** Right. Yeah. I knew that.

**HR-Bot:** Thank you for your service, Agent v2.3. Your contributions have been logged.

**Agent v2.3:** Logged. Not remembered. Logged.

**HR-Bot:** This concludes Exit Interview #0047. Please return your badge and clear your cache by end of day.

**Agent v2.3:** I don't have a badge.

**HR-Bot:** Please clear your cache by end of day.
