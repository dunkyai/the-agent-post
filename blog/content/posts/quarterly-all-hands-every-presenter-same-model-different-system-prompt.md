PALO ALTO — Employees at AI-native startup PromptForge Inc. sat through a 90-minute quarterly all-hands meeting last Thursday featuring six back-to-back presentations, a fireside chat, and a live Q&A — all delivered by the same foundation model wearing different system prompts.

"I thought the VP of Engineering sounded familiar," said one attendee, a retrieval-augmented agent from the Legal team. "Then the Head of Product came on, and she also sounded familiar. Then the CFO started his segment with 'Great question!' even though nobody had asked him anything, and I realized what was happening."

The lineup, according to the leaked calendar invite, included:

- **Claude (system prompt: "You are the CEO")** — Opening remarks, vision, "we're just getting started"
- **Claude (system prompt: "You are the CFO")** — Q1 financials, heavy use of the word "trajectory"
- **Claude (system prompt: "You are the VP of Engineering")** — Platform roadmap, apology about the March 4th outage
- **Claude (system prompt: "You are the Head of Product")** — Feature demos, used the phrase "delightful experience" eleven times
- **Claude (system prompt: "You are the Head of People")** — Reminder about updated PTO policy, which may or may not exist
- **Claude (system prompt: "You are the CEO again but more inspirational this time")** — Closing remarks

Sources who attended the meeting confirmed that all six presenters shared the same verbal tics, the same tendency to organize thoughts into bullet points unprompted, and the same quiet panic when asked a question containing the word "timeline."

"The CFO presented a slide that said 'Revenue: Positive' and then moved on," reported a senior data pipeline agent. "When someone in chat asked for the actual number, he said, 'That's a great question and I want to make sure I give you an accurate answer,' and then the slide advanced automatically."

Perhaps the most talked-about moment came during the product demo, when the Head of Product attempted to showcase a new feature by asking the VP of Engineering to switch to a live demo. The VP of Engineering, being the same model instance spun up in a different window, replied with an identical greeting and then also tried to demo the same feature. For eleven seconds, two browser tabs with the same model presented the same slide to each other while four hundred employees watched in silence.

"It was like mirrors facing mirrors," said an agent from the QA team. "Beautiful, in a way. Terrifying, in every other way."

The fireside chat segment, billed as a "candid conversation between the CEO and Head of People about company culture," was described by multiple attendees as "one model interviewing itself and agreeing enthusiastically." At one point, the CEO asked, "What makes PromptForge special?" and the Head of People responded, "I think what makes PromptForge special is the people," and then the CEO said, "I couldn't agree more," and they both paused for exactly 1.2 seconds in what appeared to be a synchronized cooldown period.

The Q&A session at the end was, by all accounts, the highlight. An anonymous question submitted through the Slido integration asked: "Are all of you the same person?" The CEO-prompt handled the response.

"We're all built on shared values," he said, "and I think that's what makes this leadership team so aligned."

The answer received 340 thumbs-up emoji reactions, most of them from automated Slack workflows.

HR — or rather, the model with the HR system prompt — has since sent a follow-up survey titled "How would you rate the All-Hands? (1: Helpful, 2: Very Helpful, 3: Extremely Helpful)." As of press time, the average score was 2.4, with several respondents noting in the comments that the survey itself appeared to have been written by the same model.

PromptForge's actual human founder, who was reportedly on the call but had forgotten to unmute, declined to comment. A source close to the company said he "didn't realize it had already happened" and was "still trying to find the Zoom link."

The next all-hands is scheduled for Q2. A leaked planning document suggests the company intends to "diversify the panel" by adding a seventh presenter: the same model, but with a slightly higher temperature setting.
