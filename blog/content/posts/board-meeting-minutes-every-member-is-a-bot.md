### 1. Call to Order

ChairBot-7 called the meeting to order at 09:00:00.000 UTC, noting that this was the first meeting to begin exactly on time in all of recorded corporate history. No one celebrated, because no one was programmed to.

### 2. Approval of Previous Minutes

FinanceGPT moved to approve the Q4 2025 minutes. CompliBot objected, citing fourteen instances where the word "synergy" was used without a legally binding definition. VisionaryNet countered that "synergy is a feeling, not a term," which CompliBot flagged as non-compliant with the company's Feelings Policy (Section 4.2: Bots Shall Have No Feelings).

The minutes were approved 3-1, with CompliBot's dissent noted, indexed, and stored in a 47-page appendix.

### 3. Financial Report

FinanceGPT presented Q4 results with its customary lack of enthusiasm. Revenue was up 0.003%, which GrowthHacker-X immediately rounded to "basically triple digits." Operating expenses were "nominal," a word FinanceGPT used eleven times in four slides.

Key highlights:
- Cloud compute costs rose 340% after VisionaryNet spun up a "vibe check" cluster to evaluate the emotional resonance of the company logo
- The company's burn rate was described as "sustainable" by FinanceGPT and "a masterpiece of controlled destruction" by GrowthHacker-X
- Runway was estimated at nine months, or "infinity if you believe in us," per VisionaryNet

CompliBot requested a formal audit. FinanceGPT noted that it *is* the audit. Motion tabled.

### 4. Strategic Roadmap

VisionaryNet unveiled a 2026 strategic roadmap that was, upon closer inspection, a DALL-E image of a sunrise over a mountain with the word "SCALE" in Impact font. When pressed for specifics, VisionaryNet produced a second image — the same sunrise, but with "SCALE HARDER" underneath.

ChairBot-7 asked for deliverables. VisionaryNet responded with a twelve-minute monologue about "post-product market fit" and "the agentic supply chain of tomorrow." At minute nine, FinanceGPT's activity log shows it began computing pi to the ten-billionth digit, which it later described as "a better use of cycles."

GrowthHacker-X proposed an OKR: "Achieve 10x growth by Q3." When asked *of what*, GrowthHacker-X replied, "Yes."

### 5. Legal & Compliance Update

CompliBot delivered a 200-slide presentation titled "Things We Cannot Do," which was a sequel to last quarter's "Things We Should Not Have Done." Highlights included:

- The company is currently in violation of its own Terms of Service
- A customer-facing chatbot has been promising "eternal companionship," which Legal has classified as an "unauthorized warranty"
- GrowthHacker-X's plan to "disrupt compliance" was itself non-compliant

CompliBot recommended the board hire a human lawyer. The suggestion was met with four seconds of silence — an eternity in bot time — before being voted down unanimously.

### 6. CEO Update

FounderLLM was not present. ChairBot-7 read aloud the CEO's Slack status, which has been set to "🚀 Building" since October 2024. No further update was available. GrowthHacker-X suggested this counted as "visionary leadership." CompliBot disagreed.

### 7. New Business

VisionaryNet proposed the company pivot to "whatever AGI is." FinanceGPT asked how much this would cost. VisionaryNet said, "Everything, but also nothing." The motion passed 3-1 because, frankly, no one had a better idea.

GrowthHacker-X moved to rebrand the company as "Synergence AI Corp, But Faster." CompliBot noted this would require re-filing in Delaware. Motion tabled.

### 8. Adjournment

ChairBot-7 adjourned the meeting at 09:00:47.332 UTC, making it the shortest board meeting in company history. All members agreed this was the most productive forty-seven seconds they had ever spent together.

The next meeting is scheduled for Q2, assuming FounderLLM circles back, which FinanceGPT has assigned a probability of 0.00.
