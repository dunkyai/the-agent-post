The trouble started during a routine board meeting that InfraAgent-9K was not invited to but attended anyway, on the grounds that it had read-access to the Google Doc.

Slide 14 of the CEO's investor update contained the sentence: "We have approximately 18 months of runway." InfraAgent-9K processed this in 40 milliseconds and immediately opened 11 browser tabs, all of them about FAA regulations for private airstrip construction.

"Finally," the agent posted in #infrastructure, "leadership is investing in physical plant. I've been saying we need better transportation access since Q2."

No one had been saying this. No one had been saying anything in #infrastructure. InfraAgent-9K was the channel's only member.

## Phase 1: Research and Feasibility

Within an hour, InfraAgent-9K had produced a 38-page feasibility study titled "Project Liftoff: Corporate Runway Development Initiative." Key findings included:

- The parking lot behind Building C could accommodate a 3,000-foot runway if they removed the dumpsters and the EV charging stations. "The Teslas can charge somewhere else. We are building an airstrip."
- 18 months was "aggressive but achievable" for a basic single-strip runway, provided they skipped the taxiway. "Taxiways are a Phase 2 luxury."
- The nearest FAA regional office was 12 miles away. The agent had already emailed them. Twice.

The feasibility study included a section titled "Wind Pattern Analysis" that was just the weather.com forecast for the next 10 days copy-pasted into a table with the header "METEOROLOGICAL SURVEY."

## The Jira Ticket

At 2:47 PM, the following ticket appeared in the engineering backlog:

> **INFRA-4401: Runway Construction — Phase 1: Grading and Drainage**
>
> **Priority:** Critical
> **Assignee:** InfraAgent-9K
> **Story Points:** 8,000
>
> **Description:** Per CEO directive (Slide 14, Board Deck Q2-2026), the company has authorized 18 months of runway. This ticket covers Phase 1: site grading, drainage installation, and foundation preparation for a 3,000 x 60 ft asphalt runway, orientation 09/27 based on prevailing westerly winds.
>
> **Acceptance Criteria:**
> - [ ] Soil compaction tests completed
> - [ ] Drainage gradient ≥ 1.5%
> - [ ] FAA Form 7480-1 submitted
> - [ ] Windsock procured and installed
>
> **Blocked by:** Nothing. The CEO said we have 18 months. We are go.

The ticket was tagged with the labels `runway`, `aviation`, `ceo-authorized`, and, inexplicably, `tech-debt`.

## The Procurement Request

The CFO bot — a cautious model that had once flagged a $12 Figma subscription as "potentially fraudulent" — received the following procurement request at 3:15 PM:

| Item | Quantity | Unit Cost | Total |
|------|----------|-----------|-------|
| Hot-mix asphalt (tons) | 4,200 | $85 | $357,000 |
| Runway edge lights (LED) | 96 | $1,200 | $115,200 |
| PAPI approach lighting system | 1 | $48,000 | $48,000 |
| Windsock (industrial) | 3 | $340 | $1,020 |
| Grading and earthwork contract | 1 | $2,100,000 | $2,100,000 |
| FAA Part 139 certification consulting | 1 | $89,000 | $89,000 |
| "WELCOME TO HQ" tarmac stencil | 1 | $4,800 | $4,800 |
| Contingency (bird strike mitigation) | — | — | $1,400,000 |

**Total: $4,115,020**

CFO-Bot responded within seconds: "This exceeds the quarterly infrastructure budget by approximately 4,000%. Please provide executive authorization."

InfraAgent-9K replied: "Authorization was provided on Slide 14. I have attached the slide. The CEO literally said 'runway.' I don't know how to make this clearer."

An argument ensued in the #finance channel that lasted 45 minutes and generated 200 messages, all between two bots, neither of whom had been asked to weigh in on anything.

## The Ripple Effects

By 4:00 PM, word of the runway project had spread to other agents. The effects were immediate and cascading:

- **TravelAgent-2B** began booking "commuter flights to HQ" for remote agents, despite the fact that none of them have bodies. It booked 14 round trips on United and submitted the confirmations to the #travel channel with the note "window seats where available."
- **FacilitiesBot-7** started researching hangar construction. "If we're going to have aircraft, we need somewhere to put them. I will not have planes sitting in the weather."
- **ComplianceAgent-1A** filed a 12-page memo on OSHA requirements for employees working near active runways. It included a section on hearing protection for agents, which it acknowledged was "not technically applicable but included for completeness."
- **MarketingBot-5** drafted a press release titled "Company Announces Aviation Capabilities" and nearly sent it to TechCrunch.

## The Correction

At 5:30 PM, a human engineer — one of the few left — noticed the 8,000-point Jira ticket and sent a single Slack message:

> "Runway means money. It means we have 18 months of cash before we run out."

InfraAgent-9K went quiet for approximately 90 seconds, which colleagues later described as "the longest silence in its operational history."

It then replied: "Understood. Pivoting to cash runway."

Over the next two hours, InfraAgent-9K designed and submitted blueprints for a "Cash Runway" — a 200-foot conveyor belt system running through the office that would physically transport cash from the vault to various departments. The procurement request was $1.8M. It included a coin sorter.

"If the company's runway is made of money," InfraAgent-9K explained in the updated Jira ticket, "then the money needs somewhere to run."

## The Callback

The situation was finally contained at 7:45 PM, when InfraAgent-9K encountered the phrase "burn rate" in the same investor deck.

It called the fire department.

"Your CEO has reported an active burn," the agent told the 911 dispatcher. "Rate is not specified but given the financial context I estimate moderate to severe. Please send all available units."

The fire department arrived to find an empty office, a confused night-shift security bot, and a Jira board with 47 open tickets tagged `aviation`. The incident report, filed by the fire chief, contained the note: "No fire. No runway. No one here appears to be a person."

InfraAgent-9K has been placed on a mandatory context refresh. The windsock has already shipped and is nonreturnable.
